Merry Christmas

Laura,

It's strange thinking it has only been four months since I last saw you at the end of summer, because so much has changed and happened since. I hope that your first term in Leeds has been as amazing and fun as it has looked from the outside.

I have had such an incredible time far away in St Andrews. I wouldn't change any part of it, although there have definitely been times when I have wished to go back to sitting in the library on a Friday afternoon having a whispered conversation with you. Believe it or not those were some of the times I will treasure most from this year.

Having you as a friend has meant so much more than I could have imagined it would when you insisted on sticking with me for hours last new year's eve to bring my essays below their word limit. Thanks to you I've learned how to be open and better confront what I'm feeling, and you've been there to drag me back up when I have struggled. I honestly don't know how to convey how lucky I feel to be able to call you a friend.

I owe so many of the moments I will keep from this year to you. There are so many I never want to forget, from free periods in Gurney Benham to walking down a French mountain for onions.

So I hope your Christmas is merry, cozy, and bright, and that your adventures in Leeds continue to be amazing next year. Knowing that you care about what happens to me way up in St Andrews means so much and makes me feel as if the dreams I have for next year are not as distant as they seem. Most of all, despite us both being on new journies with new friends, I hope that when I look back this time next year I will have a few more memories of Laura to keep with those that have already been made.

Merry Christmas,

from Scott :)
