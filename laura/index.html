Merry Christmas

Laura,

It's strange thinking it has only been four months since I last saw you at the end of summer, because so much has happened since. I hope that your first term in Leeds has been as amazing and fun as it has looked.

I have had an incredible time far away in St Andrews. I wouldn't change any part of it, although there have been times I've wished to go back to the library on a Friday afternoon and whisper a conversation with you.

Having you as a friend has meant so much more than I could have imagined when you stuck with me for hours last new year's eve to get my essays below the word limit. Thanks to you I've learned to be open and confront what I'm feeling, and you've been there to pull me up when I have struggled. I don't know how to convey how lucky I feel to be able to call you a friend.

I owe so many of the moments I will keep from this year to you, from chatting in free periods to walking down a French mountain for onions, I will treasure them all.

So I hope your Christmas is cozy and warm, and that your adventures in Leeds continue to be amazing next year. Knowing that you care makes my own dreams for next year feel so much closer. Most of all, I hope I will have a few more memories of Laura to keep amongst those from this year.

Merry Christmas,

from Scott :)
